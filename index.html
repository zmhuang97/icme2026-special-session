ğŸ”¹ Overview

The landscape of multimedia is undergoing a paradigm shift driven by the convergence of Generative AI (e.g., diffusion models, autoregressive LLMs) and Agentic Systems (e.g., tool-using, planning, multi-agent collaboration). These technologies are reshaping visual data compression, processing, and evaluation. Traditional signal processing is evolving toward model-centric paradigms, where large models act as priors for ultra-low bitrate coding and enable autonomous workflow orchestration. This session explores how to bridge the gap between generative intelligence and practical multimedia systems.

ğŸ”¹ Topics of Interest

Representative topics include, but are not limited to:

  • Generative Coding & Processing: diffusion/LLM-driven reconstruction, enhancement, compression.
  • Agentic Media Workflows: multi-agent generation, autonomous pipelines, tool-oriented media systems.
  • Efficient Representations: semantics-aware tokens, prompt optimization, cross-modal embeddings.
  • Evaluation & Benchmarking: perceptual metrics, controllability, safety of AI-generated content.

ğŸ”¹ Organizer Biographies

Prof. Shiqi Wang

Shiqi Wang (Senior Member, IEEE) is a Professor at the Department of Computer Science, City University of Hong Kong. He received his Ph.D. from Peking University in 2014. An active contributor to standardization, he has proposed over 70 technical proposals to ISO/MPEG, ITU-T, and AVS. He has authored more than 400 refereed papers, including over 100 in IEEE Transactions. His research spans semantic/visual communication, AI-generated content (AIGC) management, and image/video quality assessment. Prof. Wang is a recipient of multiple Best Paper Awards (IEEE VCIP 2019, ICME 2019, IEEE Multimedia 2018, PCM 2017) and the IEEE ICIP 2018 Best Student Paper Award. He serves/served as an Associate Editor for IEEE TCSVT, TMM, TIP, and TCYB, and notably served as the TPC Chair of ICME 2024.

Prof. Chuanmin Jia

Chuanmin Jia (Member, IEEE) is currently a tenure-track Assistant Professor at Wangxuan Institute of Computer Technology, Peking University. His research interests include intelligent video compression and processing. He is the recipient of the ACM SIGMM China Rising Star, Best Paper Award of PCM 2017 and IEEE MM 2018, Best Student Paper Award of IEEE MIPR 2019, and video-track Winner of Challenge on Learned Image Compression in IEEE DCC 2024. He serves as an Associate Editor for IEEE Transactions on Circuits and Systems for Video Technology and elected member of IEEE MSA and VSPC technical committee.

Prof. Qi Mao

Qi Mao is a Professor at the Communication University of China. She holds a Ph.D. from Peking University and has enriched her research background through appointments at UC Merced and the National University of Singapore. Her expertise lies in intelligent media content generation and coding, with works published in leading venues such as IEEE TIP/TMM, CVPR, IJCV, ECCV, and SIGCOMM. Prof. Mao is highly active in the academic community, currently serving as an Area Chair for ICCV 2025, WACV 2026, CVPR 2026, and ICML 2026, and as the Short Paper and Demo Paper Co-Chair for ACM MM Asia 2025.

Dr. Zhimeng Huang

Zhimeng Huang is a Boya Postdoctoral Fellow in the Department of Computer Science at Peking University, where he also received his Ph.D. in 2024. His research interests include image and video compression, coding for machine, and machine learning. He has made active contributions to international and national standards, submitting more than 20 technical proposals to ISO/MPEG and AVS. Dr. Huang has published extensively in top-tier venues such as IEEE TIP, IEEE TCSVT, CVPR, and AAAI.
